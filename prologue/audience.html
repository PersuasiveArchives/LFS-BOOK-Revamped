ii. Audience

There are many reasons to read this book, especially if you're wondering why you'd build a Linux system from scratch instead of using an existing one.

One key reason is to understand how Linux works. By building your own LFS system, you'll see how everything fits together and depends on each other. This also lets you customize your system to meet your needs.

LFS gives you full control over your system, without relying on someone else's setup. You make all the decisions about your system.

LFS also helps you create lightweight systems. Unlike other distributions that come with unnecessary software, LFS lets you build only what you need, which is great for small systems like bootable CDs, USB sticks, and embedded devices.

Security is another advantage. By compiling everything from source, you can check and apply security patches yourself, ensuring everything is secure.

The goal of Linux From Scratch is to help you build a complete system from scratch. Even if you don't want to do this, the knowledge in this book can still be valuable.

There are many reasons to build your own LFS system, but education is the most important. Through LFS, you'll gain powerful knowledge about Linux.
